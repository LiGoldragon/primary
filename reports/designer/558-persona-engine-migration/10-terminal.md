## Correction to the seed

The audit-556 seed flagged "concurrency-1 starvation," but `config.rs:87` now returns `RequestConcurrencyLimit::new(64)` and the substrate (`async_runtime.rs:259`) is a Semaphore-gated RequestGate actor — that starvation defect is fixed. The supervisor-lacks-RestartPolicy, leaked-supervision-thread, and redb-inline defects all remain (axis 7).

## Migration path for terminal

terminal is one of the furthest-along components: the supervisor process is already the schema-rust-next-emitted shell on triad-runtime's actor-native (RequestGate-actor + listener-task) substrate, with signal-executor fully gone and a real sema-engine store. Three threads of work remain, none gated on missing substrate except the runner. First, harden the existing kameo actors — add `RestartPolicy`, join (don't leak) the supervision thread, and push redb onto `spawn_blocking`; these are local edits. Second, when schema-rust-next ships the nexus Runner glue, replace the `supervisor.ask` direct dispatch with `NexusWork -> Runner::drive -> NexusAction` so SEMA and terminal-cell effects become typed actions (the nexus enums are already emitted and waiting). Third, derive `signal-terminal` and `meta-signal-terminal` from schema-next to retire `signal_channel!`, and fold the hand-written per-session PTY daemon (the last flag-parsing, blocking-loop process) under the supervisor so the whole component honors the single-binary-argument rule.
