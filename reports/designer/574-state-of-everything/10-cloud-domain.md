### Findings

**The daemons are real, not scaffold.** `cloud` carries a genuine Cloudflare provider: `cloud/src/cloudflare.rs` defines `HttpApi` over `ureq` against `https://api.cloudflare.com/client/v4` with real GET/POST/PATCH/DELETE, a `CredentialSource` trait and `EnvironmentCredentialSource`. Both daemons implement `ComponentDaemon` (`CloudDaemon`, `DomainCriomeDaemon`) over the schema-rust-next-emitted daemon spine and wire both ordinary and meta inputs (`handle_ordinary_input`/`handle_meta_input` at `domain-criome/src/lib.rs:301,312`; `handle_schema_ordinary_input`/`handle_schema_meta_input` at `cloud/src/lib.rs:636,644`). Zero `todo!`/`unimplemented!` markers anywhere in the cluster.

**No repo defines its own kameo actors.** The actor machinery (`RequestGate`, `ActorMultiListenerDaemon`, the `kameo` re-export) lives in `triad-runtime` (`triad-runtime/src/async_runtime.rs`). These components are thin typed hooks over the emitted spine plus the library-provided actor runtime — a "real daemon, library-owned actors" shape, not a per-repo actor system.

**The contract repos are exemplary.** `signal-cloud`, `meta-signal-cloud`, `signal-domain-criome`, `meta-signal-domain-criome` each consist of a handwritten `lib.rs` of typed nouns + impls and a generated `src/schema/lib.rs`. They have zero module-level free functions, zero fake-NOTA, and route all encoding through the derived `nota_next::NotaEncode`/`NotaDecode`. Generated share runs 71-85% of production LOC. The handwritten `to_nota` in generated code delegates to `<Self as NotaEncode>::to_nota` — correct, not flaggable.

**Dependency staleness is uniform and the d8862b6 hazard does not bite.** All six repos pin identical foundation revs in Cargo.lock: schema-next `77e71a4` (5 behind HEAD), schema-rust-next `a35eff7` (20 behind), nota-next `f0e435a` (8 behind), triad-runtime `0b1746d` (10 behind), signal-frame `904bb2f` (4 behind). **No consumer is on nota-next `d8862b6`** (the encoding bump). They pin `f0e435a`, which is 4 commits *older* than the `ae5c25cd` point schema-rust-next HEAD deliberately isolates at — so the generated code and the nota-next codec are paired consistently and there is no encoding-format mismatch in this cluster. `flake.lock` in every repo pins only Nix build tooling (crane, fenix, flake-utils, nixpkgs, rust-analyzer), NOT the foundation Rust crates, so there is no Cargo-vs-flake disagreement on foundation pins; the domain-criome triad simply has a slightly newer nixpkgs than the cloud triad (build-tooling drift).

**Schema migration debt sits on the daemon plane.** The live source of truth everywhere is the split-triad form (`nexus.schema`+`sema.schema` for daemons, `lib.schema` for contracts) — confirmed by `build.rs`, which references only those. But the daemon schemas are still overwhelmingly OLD verbose self-tag: `cloud/schema/nexus.schema` 19/19, `cloud/schema/sema.schema` 23/25, `domain-criome/schema/nexus.schema` 11/11, `domain-criome/schema/sema.schema` 20/23 repeated-name `(X X)` tags that 52ro now writes terse as `(X)` or direct (`(Continue NexusWork)`). The contracts are further along: `signal-cloud` 6/29 verbose, `signal-domain-criome` 3/14, both meta-signals 0. No `Bytes`/hash leaves are in play, so yp29/yp29 and lm84 are N/A for this cluster.

**Stale `.concept.schema` cruft in three repos.** `signal-cloud/schema/signal-cloud.concept.schema` (May 24), `meta-signal-cloud/schema/owner-signal-cloud.concept.schema` (Jun 4), and `signal-domain-criome/schema/signal-domain-criome.concept.schema` (May 24) are all OLD single-concept form (with the qz6j-broken alias-transparency pattern `Text (String)` and redundant `(Provider Provider)` self-tags) and are referenced nowhere — not by `build.rs`, not anywhere in source. Pure orphaned cruft. `meta-signal-domain-criome` already deleted its concept file and is the cluster's cleanest schema state — the model the others should follow.
