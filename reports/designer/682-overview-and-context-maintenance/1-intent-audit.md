## Flagged maintenance candidates (recommend only)

No removals, no contradictions, no duplicates requiring a merge. Four
low-stakes refinements; (1) is the only one worth acting on soon.

1. **w2g3 — Clarify or ChangeCertainty (resolved-open-question lineage).**
   w2g3 records the criome-auth envelope as "still open" at `Medium`.
   2st7 then explicitly states it "settles the envelope design left open
   by w2g3" at `High`. w2g3 is not wrong (it's the question that 2st7
   answered) but a fresh reader hits an active `Medium` "open" record
   beside its `High` resolution. Recommend a one-line `Clarify` on w2g3
   noting the envelope is now settled by 2st7 (keeps lineage visible),
   rather than retire — the pilot-consumer intent itself still stands.
   Not a contradiction; a stale "still open" qualifier. Psyche
   authorization not strictly required since this is transcription/status
   cleanup, but route it through the guardian.

2. **p3td — consider BumpImportance.** p3td is the canonical definition
   of what criome *is* (the supersessor of obuf, the root the other 14
   records elaborate) yet sits at `Importance Minimum` like its
   downstream details. Importance drives retrieval order; the root
   definition out-ranking its elaborations is the intended use of the
   ladder. Recommend `BumpImportance p3td` (and optionally pviw, the
   Telos name). Low stakes; importance only, not certainty.

3. **m0p2 / p3td overlap — no action, monitor.** Both open with "criome
   is/`'`s ... universal agreement machine for authorization(s)." This is
   restatement-as-framing, not duplication: p3td defines the organ +
   quorum primitive; m0p2 defines the pulse/heartbeat mechanism. They do
   not contradict and neither is subsumed. No merge. Noting it so a
   future sweep does not re-flag it as a dup.

4. **eeeo vs p3td/m0p2 — no overlap.** eeeo is about the signal-standard
   schema crate and ComponentKind roster; orthogonal to the
   agreement-machine semantics. No action.
