# 497 - Certainty recalibration and the intent-maintenance worklist

## The directive

The psyche, 2026-06-04: *"A lot of intent is captured as Maximum, which
is really strong and should be rare... let's make a ladder, a benchmark
of what a Medium / High / Maximum situation looks like... do a pass on
the Spirit certainty and dial it down... find out what is actually
Maximum, what is not... the default should be Medium. What am I, some
Olympian god who knows with absolute certainty every thing I say?"*

Captured as record **1612** (Principle, High): certainty must be
calibrated against a benchmark ladder; Maximum is rare; Medium is the
default; certainty is judged from the psyche's actual conviction, and
agents have been over-rating it. Rated High, not Maximum — practising
the discipline it describes.

## The benchmark ladder

Built into `skills/intent-log.md` §"The ladder — what situation each
level is for". The spine, judging the psyche's **conviction** (not the
topic's importance):

- **Maximum** — a universal founding rule that could stand as a founding
  axiom of the whole way of working, or one the psyche told us to put in
  essence. Rare; most sessions capture none. Test: would this still be a
  founding rule a year from now across every repo and role?
- **VeryHigh** — very firm, near-irreversible, but scope-limited to one
  discipline (e.g. landed-means-main).
- **High** — a real decision the psyche committed to. The normal home
  for a decision.
- **Medium** — the default. A preference or direction without strong
  emphasis. When in doubt, Medium.
- **Low / VeryLow / Minimum** — tentative to barely-above-noise.
- **Zero** — the recoverable removal marker (superseded, duplicate,
  mis-captured).

Two framings made explicit: *the psyche is not an omniscient god, so
Maximum cannot be the reflex*, and *importance is not certainty*. The
`keep-working` skill (record 1614) landed in the same session.
