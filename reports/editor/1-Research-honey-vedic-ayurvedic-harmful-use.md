# 1 — Honey harmful-use passages in Vedic and Ayurvedic sources

## Finding

If "Vedas" means the Saṃhitā hymn layer strictly, I did not find a
source passage that says bee honey is harmful in a particular use. The
Vedic passages I found use `madhu` as sweetness, honey, nectar, or
auspicious delight.

The concrete harmful-use rules come from classical Ayurveda, especially
Caraka Saṃhitā Sūtrasthāna 26 and 27. Those passages warn against heated
honey, honey for a heat-afflicted person, equal honey and ghee, hot water
after honey, and several honey-containing food combinations classified
as `viruddha` (incompatible).

This is textual research, not medical advice.

## Source Boundary

The working distinction:

- Vedic hymn evidence: Ṛgveda 1.90.6-8, a sweetness blessing using
  `madhu`.
- Ayurvedic harmful-use evidence: Caraka Saṃhitā Sūtrasthāna 26.84,
  26.90, and 27.245-248.

Some modern wellness writing calls the Ayurveda rules "Vedic." The
source evidence does not support that wording unless "Vedic" is being
used loosely for the broader Sanskritic medical tradition.

## Vedic Baseline: Madhu As Auspicious Sweetness

Ṛgveda 1.90.6:

> `madhu vātā ṛtāyate madhu kṣaranti sindhavaḥ | mādhvīr naḥ santv oṣadhīḥ ||`

English, editor rendering:

> Sweet may the winds be for the one aligned with ṛta; sweet may the
> rivers flow; sweet may the plants be for us.

Ṛgveda 1.90.7:

> `madhu naktam utoṣaso madhumat pārthivaṃ rajaḥ | madhu dyaur astu naḥ pitā ||`

English, editor rendering:

> Sweet may night and dawn be; honey-sweet may the earthly realm be;
> sweet may father heaven be for us.

Ṛgveda 1.90.8:

> `madhumān no vanaspatir madhumām̐ astu sūryaḥ | mādhvīr gāvo bhavantu naḥ ||`

English, editor rendering:

> Honey-sweet may the forest tree be for us; honey-sweet may the sun be;
> honey-sweet may the cows be for us.

The Vedic baseline matters because it prevents a false citation. These
verses support honey/sweetness as beneficent imagery, not a list of
dangerous honey uses.

## Caraka Saṃhitā: Harmful Honey Uses

### Heated Honey / Honey For A Heat-Afflicted Person

Caraka Saṃhitā Sūtrasthāna 27.246:

> `hanyān madhūṣṇam uṣṇārtam athavā saviṣānvayāt |`

English, editor rendering:

> Heated honey, or honey given to one afflicted by heat, may kill,
> because of its association with poison.

Caraka repeats the same warning in Sūtrasthāna 26.84:

> `madhu coṣṇam uṣṇārtasya ca madhu maraṇāya |`

English, editor rendering:

> Hot honey, and honey for one afflicted by heat, leads to death.

The attached Ayurvedadīpikā comment on Sūtrasthāna 26 explains the logic
this way:

> `yato viṣānvayaṃ madhu viṣasya coṣṇavirodhi`

English, editor rendering:

> Honey has an association with poison, and poison is incompatible with
> heat.

### Honey-Induced Āma

Caraka Saṃhitā Sūtrasthāna 27.247-248:

> `nātaḥ kaṣṭatamaṃ kiñcin madhvāmāt tad dhi mānavam |`
>
> `upakramavirodhitvāt sadyo hanyād yathā viṣam ||`
>
> `āme soṣṇā kriyā kāryā sā madhvāme virudhyate |`
>
> `madhvāmaṃ dāruṇaṃ tasmāt sadyo hanyād yathā viṣam ||`

English, editor rendering:

> Nothing is more difficult than honey-caused āma in a human. Because
> its treatment conflicts with the needed procedure, it may kill quickly
> like poison.
>
> In āma, hot treatment is to be done; but that conflicts with honey-āma.
> Therefore honey-āma is severe and may kill quickly like poison.

### Equal Honey And Ghee

Caraka Saṃhitā Sūtrasthāna 26.90:

> `madhusarpiḥ samadhṛtaṃ mātrayā tad virudhyate ||`

English, editor rendering:

> Honey and ghee in equal measure are incompatible by dose.

Sūtrasthāna 26.84 gives the same item in list form:

> `madhusarpiṣī samadhṛte ... viruddhāni`

English, editor rendering:

> Honey and ghee in equal quantity are among the incompatible items.

### Hot Water After Honey

Caraka Saṃhitā Sūtrasthāna 26.84:

> `madhu pītvā uṣṇodakaṃ ... viruddhāni`

English, editor rendering:

> Hot water after drinking honey is among the incompatible items.

### Equal Honey And Rainwater

Caraka Saṃhitā Sūtrasthāna 26.84:

> `madhu vāri cāntarikṣaṃ samadhṛtaṃ ... viruddhāni`

English, editor rendering:

> Honey and rainwater in equal measure are among the incompatible items.

### Honey With Lotus Seed

Caraka Saṃhitā Sūtrasthāna 26.84:

> `madhu puṣkarabījaṃ ... viruddhāni`

English, editor rendering:

> Honey with lotus seed is among the incompatible items.

### Kākamācī With Honey

Caraka Saṃhitā Sūtrasthāna 26.84:

> `kākamācī madhu ca maraṇāya`

English, editor rendering:

> Kākamācī with honey is fatal.

The plant identification in the online translation is black nightshade
(`Solanum nigrum`), but the report keeps the Sanskrit name because plant
identifications can vary by edition and materia-medica tradition.

### Pigeon Or Certain Potherbs With Honey And Milk

Caraka Saṃhitā Sūtrasthāna 26.84:

> `na pauṣkaraṃ rohiṇīkaṃ śākaṃ kapotān vā sarṣapatailabhraṣṭān madhupayobhyāṃ sahābhyavaharet`

English, editor rendering:

> One should not eat pauṣkara or rohiṇīka potherb, or pigeon fried in
> mustard oil, together with honey and milk.

The following prose in the same passage gives the consequence as serious
circulatory and throat disorders, epilepsy, or death.

### Jātuka Or Ripe Nikuca With Honey And Milk

Caraka Saṃhitā Sūtrasthāna 26.84:

> `na jātukaśākaṃ na nikucaṃ pakvaṃ madhupayobhyāṃ sahopayojyam`

English, editor rendering:

> Jātuka potherb, and ripe nikuca, should not be used together with
> honey and milk.

The same passage says this can cause death, loss of strength, complexion,
vitality, sexual potency, or severe disease.

## Compact Rule List

From the sourced Caraka passages, the harmful honey uses are:

- heated honey;
- honey given to someone afflicted by heat;
- honey involved in honey-caused āma;
- honey and ghee in equal quantity;
- hot water after drinking honey;
- honey and rainwater in equal quantity;
- honey with lotus seed;
- kākamācī with honey;
- pauṣkara or rohiṇīka potherb, or pigeon fried in mustard oil, with
  honey and milk;
- jātuka potherb or ripe nikuca with honey and milk.
