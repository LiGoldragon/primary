## Signal / Signal-Core Understanding

The working distinction is now:

- `/git/github.com/LiGoldragon/signal-core` is the domain-free wire kernel: frame envelope, request/reply mechanics, length-prefixed rkyv framing, handshake/versioning, `SignalVerb`, `signal_channel!`, and generic request payload conversion.
- `/git/github.com/LiGoldragon/signal` is a sema/criome vocabulary crate over the kernel, not the kernel itself. It owns native records such as operation records, record bundles, flow graph records, diagnostics, hashes, and translation-adjacent sema/criome vocabulary.

Direct users of the `signal` crate found during the scan:

- `/git/github.com/LiGoldragon/nexus`
- `/git/github.com/LiGoldragon/mentci-lib`
- `/git/github.com/LiGoldragon/mentci-egui`
- `/git/github.com/LiGoldragon/signal-forge`

Persona does not currently depend on the `signal` crate. Persona-side contracts use `signal-core` plus the `signal-persona-*` contract crates.

Current `criome` also does not use the `signal` crate directly; it uses `signal-core` and `signal-criome`. That is a useful warning that `signal` may still contain older sema/criome vocabulary that should be audited before treating it as current authority.
