## The extension — six verified peer-reviewed pillars

A new source-extract, `TheBookOfSol/source-extracts/Raw_Vegan_Fruitarian_Harm.md`, compiles the modern clinical and forensic record, each citation in house format with an Āyurvedic reading beside it. The spine is the Giessen Raw Food Study — the largest cohort ever assembled — plus the bone and B-12 literature and the forensic cases:

- **Koebnick et al. 1999** (*Ann Nutr Metab* 43(2):69–79) — weight loss 9.9 kg (men) / 12 kg (women); underweight in 15–25%; **~30% of women under 45 partially-to-completely amenorrheic**, dose-dependent on percent-raw. The *dhātu-kṣaya* / *ārtava-kṣaya* curve drawn in human subjects.
- **Koebnick et al. 2005** (*J Nutr* 135(10):2372–2378) — **38% vitamin B-12 deficient**, 46% low HDL, elevated homocysteine; deficiency lifts only with outside supplementation. The blood-marker of a transformation *agni* could not finish.
- **Ganss et al. 1999** (*Caries Res* 33(1):74–80) — only **2.3% of 130 raw-fooders free of dental erosion** (vs 13.2% of controls); 60.5% with severe erosion; median 4.8 citrus servings/day. *Amla-rasa atiyoga*, written in enamel.
- **Fontana et al. 2005** (*Arch Intern Med* 165(6):684–689) — low bone mineral density in raw vegans. **Carried with its caveat**: normal turnover markers, high vitamin D, low leptin; the authors decline to call it osteoporotic. Cited as bounded *asthi-dhātu-kṣaya*, not as proof of fracture.
- **Pawlak et al. 2013** (*Nutr Rev* 71(2):110–117) — B-12 deficiency prevalence across vegetal-only diets: 62% of pregnant women, 25–86% of children, worst in vegans and lifelong adherents. The structural debt of the exclusion.
- **Cundiff & Harris 2006** (*Nutrition Journal* 5:1) — forensic case, emaciated infant on raw vegan formula, thymus absent. **Cited honestly**: the authors themselves dispute the malnutrition cause, so the paper documents the facts while declining the verdict.

Plus the verified anecdotes: Ashton Kutcher's pancreatitis (his own account, *Hollywood Reporter* 2013) and the O'Leary murder conviction (Florida, 2019 death / 2022 life sentence — causation adjudicated by a jury).

## The verification findings — four problems in the existing articles

The deep dive turned up genuine citation faults, of the same kind the prior session caught in Ghee. These are **recommendations**; I have not edited the published article bodies, since changing citations and softening claims in finished essays is a content decision for the psyche. Each fix is exact and ready to apply on a word.

### 1. "mitāhāraṃ vinā yastu… kiñcid yogo na sidhyati" is Gheraṇḍa Saṃhitā 5.16, not HYP 1.15

Cited as *Haṭha Yoga Pradīpikā* 1.15 in **two** places — `diet/Yogis_Dont_Eat_Fruit.md` (the closing block, ~line 152) and `source-extracts/Fruit_as_Medicine.md` (§V, ~line 261). The project's own HYP extract proves the conflict: `source-extracts/Hatha_Yoga_Pradipika/quotes.md` lists HYP 1.15 as the *six-destroyers* verse ("Overeating, exertion, talkativeness…"). The "without moderate diet, no yoga" verse is **Gheraṇḍa Saṃhitā 5.16**, confirmed against the Gheraṇḍa text. Fix: change the attribution to *Gheraṇḍa Saṃhitā* 5.16 in both files.

### 2. "kṣutpipāsā kṣayaṁ yāti" attributed to bare HYP is unsound

In `diet/Fruitarianism.md` ("Where Hunger Ends", ~line 27), attributed only to *Haṭha Yoga Pradīpikā*. The phrase is not in the project's HYP extract, and "cessation of hunger and thirst" has its canonical home in **Yoga Sūtra 3.31** (*kaṇṭhakūpe kṣutpipāsā-nivṛttiḥ*); the idea recurs across the Yogabīja, Śiva Saṃhitā, and Śāṇḍilya Upaniṣad, but not as this exact line in HYP. Fix: re-attribute to *Yoga Sūtra* 3.31, or mark it "proverbial formulation, after the yogic *kṣut-pipāsā-nivṛtti* doctrine" per the house paraphrase rule.

### 3. "āhāraḥ prāṇānāṁ mūlam" is a paraphrase, and the real verse is better

In `diet/Fruitarianism.md` (~line 9), bare-attributed to *Caraka Saṃhitā*. The Sanskrit is a distillation, not a verbatim pāda. The genuine, locatable verse saying exactly this is **Caraka Sūtrasthāna 27.349–350** in the project's own digest: *"Food is the vital breath of living beings… complexion, voice, life, intellect — all these are dependent on food."* (verbatim incipit *prāṇāḥ prāṇabhṛtām annam…*). Fix: swap in 27.349–350 with the real text and locus, or mark the present line "proverbial formulation, after *Caraka Saṃhitā* Sū. 27.349." Bonus verse available for the agni argument: **Sū. 27.342** — "On agni depend strength, health, life-span and vital breath."

### 4. The Steve Jobs causal claim is unsupported

`diet/Yogis_Dont_Eat_Fruit.md` ("What Happens to People Who Try", ~line 136): "Jobs himself… died of pancreatic cancer — disease in the organ most burdened by the diet." Jobs died of a pancreatic **neuroendocrine (islet-cell) tumor**, which has no established dietary cause and no demonstrated tie to fruit or sugar. The causal insinuation should be dropped; the biographical facts (most famous fruitarian; Kutcher's pancreatitis while imitating him) stay. Two further details in the same paragraph are unverified as stated: the "nine-month-old girl… fed only fruit" matches no case I could locate (the verified pediatric cases are O'Leary's 18-month-old boy and the Cundiff-Harris 5½-month-old infant), and the "fruitarian in a psychiatric unit" anecdote is a real *mechanism* (B-12/thiamine-deficiency psychosis, reversible on refeeding) without a located individual source. Recommendation: replace the unverifiable specifics with the verified cases and the verified mechanism — the section gets stronger, not weaker.
