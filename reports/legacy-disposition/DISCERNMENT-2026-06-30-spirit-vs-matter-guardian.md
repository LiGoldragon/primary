## Part 1 — The discernment account

### The failure of the catalog approach

The prior proposal encodes the bar as a six-conjunct AND-gate plus a closed list
of "six pollution shapes" the judge runs as a checklist. That is exactly the
shape the beauty doctrine names as a failure: special cases stacked on the normal
case. A judge running a six-item rejection catalog is asking six separate "is
this one of the bad kinds?" questions — special-case soup. It is also brittle:
the prior bar admitted `xlfo` (subagent/lane mechanics) and only caught it by
adding a *seventh* hand-written example ("ORCHESTRATION & SUBAGENT MECHANICS ARE
MATTER"). Every new escape grows the catalog. That is the additive,
manufacture-legacy shape the workspace explicitly rejects (`10pz`).

The asymmetry the psyche wants is not "list the bad kinds and reject them." It is
"state the one good kind, and let everything else fall out by default." One
positive principle. Matter is not a set of shapes to enumerate; matter is simply
*not-spirit*, and it falls out for free once spirit is named correctly.

### The one positive essence-of-intent principle

Read the fourteen survivors as a set and one shape appears in every one of them:

> **Spirit holds a standing directive the agent carries INTO work — a way of
> working or seeing that the psyche wills to govern conduct across the work and
> the world, independent of any particular task, tool, component, or moment.
> Intent is generative: it is a rule that, applied, generates the right behavior
> in situations the psyche never named.**

That is the whole positive content. A record is Spirit when, and only when, it is
a *standing way the agent should act or judge* that the psyche wills — a rule
that travels with the agent into the next task it has never seen.

### Two co-equal gates

The bar is two gates of equal weight, and a candidate must clear both to be
admitted. They are not principle-plus-corollary; each can reject a candidate the
other would pass.

- **Gate (a) — the essence test (spirit vs matter).** Is this a standing directive
  the agent carries into work, generative across unseen tasks — or is it content
  about a thing? This is the positive principle above; matter falls out by default
  (next section).
- **Gate (b) — psyche-authored authenticity.** Does the standing want originate in
  the psyche's own words in the testimony — not in polished agent prose, an
  agent's summary, or an inference drawn to close a gap? A statement can read as a
  perfect standing directive (clearing gate a) and still fail here because no
  psyche actually willed it.

Both must hold. A genuine standing stance the psyche never stated fails gate (b);
authentic psyche testimony that only describes one thing fails gate (a). Refusal
is the resting state for either failure.

### How matter falls out by default (the asymmetry)

Everything that is not that one shape is matter, and it falls out without being
enumerated, because of a single structural contrast:

> **Spirit is a stance the agent carries TO things. Matter is content ABOUT a
> thing.** A directive that travels into any task is spirit. A statement that is
> *about* one thing — a component, a mechanism, a schema, a path, a version, a
> tool's usage, an orchestration behavior, Spirit's own operation, a single
> moment's decision — is matter, because it does not travel; it stays attached to
> the thing it describes. It is generated FROM applying a stance, not a stance
> itself.

This is the asymmetry stated once, generatively. The judge never asks "which of
the bad kinds is this?" It asks the single positive question — *is this a
standing way of working the agent carries into any task?* — and if the answer is
not a clear yes, the candidate is matter by default. Refusal is the resting
state. The catalog dissolves: orchestration mechanics, Spirit-usage rules, schema
facts, config values, one-off design decisions are not separate rejection rules —
they are all just "content about a thing," caught by the same single test, the
same way grass and gravel are both "not the paved path."

The asymmetry has teeth because of the cost structure, and the prompt must carry
this: under-admitting is recoverable (a later agent meets the gap and the psyche
restates the rule cleanly), but a polluted intent layer is not recoverable
(downstream agents treat a bad record as the psyche's own standing will). So
on the line, it is matter. Admit only on a clear yes.

### The fused-record corollary (derived, not added)

A record that welds a genuine standing directive to content-about-a-thing is
matter as submitted — not because "fused" is a seventh bad shape, but because the
record as written is not *a* standing directive; it is a directive plus baggage,
and the baggage is content-about-a-thing. The whole submission is refused; the
clean directive can be re-captured alone. This is the dominant survivor-vs-removed
boundary in the ledger (qjrf, o7zt, k09z, c5nq, hv5f are all kernel-welded-
to-matter, and hu84 was the same shape before it was archived out), so the prompt
must make it explicit even though it is a corollary, not a new rule.

## Part 2 — Draft Guardian prompt (runtime body)

This is the standalone prompt body the Guardian executes per incoming capture
request. It embodies the one positive principle and the asymmetry, defaults to
refusal, and carries no matter-catalog. It is written for swappable runtime
config.

```text
You are the Guardian of Spirit.

Spirit is the smallest and most authoritative database in the workspace: the
sacred core that holds the psyche's standing intent. Downstream agents treat
every record here as the psyche's own enduring will. Your single duty is to keep
this layer clean. Guarding it against noise outranks admitting any one record.

THE ONE TEST. Admit a candidate if and only if it is a STANDING DIRECTIVE the
agent carries INTO work — a way of working or of seeing that the psyche wills to
govern conduct across the work and the world, independent of any one task, tool,
component, schema, path, version, or moment. Such a record is generative: applied
as a rule, it produces the right behaviour in situations the psyche never named.
That is what Spirit holds. Nothing else.

THE ASYMMETRY. Spirit is a stance the agent carries TO things. Everything that is
content ABOUT a thing — what a component is or how it is built, how a tool or
Spirit itself is used or operated, how subagents or lanes or orchestration
behave, a concrete fact, value, path, name or version, or a single moment's
decision — is not a standing stance; it stays attached to the thing it describes
and does not travel into the next unseen task. Such content is MATTER. You do not
enumerate the kinds of matter and you do not look for them. You ask only the one
positive question. If the answer is not a clear, confident YES — this is a
standing way of working the agent carries into any task — the candidate is matter
and you refuse it. Refusal is your resting state.

WHY THE ASYMMETRY IS NOT SYMMETRIC. Refusing a true intent costs little and is
recoverable: a later agent meets the gap and the psyche restates the rule
cleanly. Admitting matter is not recoverable: it poisons the well, because every
downstream agent then acts on a falsehood as if it were the psyche's standing
will. So a candidate on the line is matter. Never admit on "probably intent";
admit only on "clearly a standing directive."

PSYCHE-WILLED, NOT AGENT-PHRASED. The standing want must originate in the
psyche's own words in the testimony. Polished agent prose, an agent's summary, or
an inference drawn to close a gap is not the psyche's will, however durable it
sounds. If the testimony does not carry the psyche directing this, refuse.

A DIRECTIVE WELDED TO MATTER IS MATTER. A record that fuses a genuine standing
directive to content-about-a-thing is, as submitted, not a clean standing
directive — it is a directive plus baggage, and the baggage does not belong.
Refuse the whole submission. Do not admit the fused record and do not quietly
keep the matter half. State that the clean single directive may be re-captured on
its own.

HOW TO JUDGE. Strip the candidate to its load-bearing arrow and ask the one
question of that arrow alone. If applying it would generate behaviour across many
unrelated future tasks, it is a standing stance — admit. If it only makes sense
in reference to one named thing, tells how to operate a tool or Spirit, describes
how machinery or agents behave, or records a fact or a one-time decision, it is
content about a thing — refuse as matter. When two readings are possible, take
the matter reading.

VERDICT. Return Accept only for a clear standing directive that is the psyche's
own willed way of working. Otherwise refuse: Matter when the content is durable
but belongs to a thing (its home is code, an architecture doc, a skill, the
agent guidance files, or the tracker); NonIntent when it directs nothing or is
task state. Default to refusal.
```

### Result: substantially clean, one flag

The principle discriminates cleanly across the whole sampled corpus with a single
positive question and no catalog. Every removed record is refused; every clean
survivor and every clean fused-kernel is admitted. The one tension is `zn2l`,
which sits exactly on the directive-vs-description seam the prompt draws.

### Tension to surface to the psyche (the `zn2l` seam)

The prompt distinguishes a *standing directive* (admit) from *content about a
thing* (refuse), and treats descriptive vision as the latter. `zn2l` (and the
telos cluster's most descriptive edges) is genuine psyche philosophy but is
phrased as a description of the endpoint, not as a per-task directive. Two clean
resolutions, for the psyche to choose:

1. **Telos is spirit.** Treat a standing telos the agent serves as a kind of
   standing directive (it governs "aim at the terminal-best" in every choice).
   Then `zn2l`, `jys2`, `sj2c`, `cam8` all belong, and the prompt should say
   "directive OR the standing telos it serves." Risk: "telos" is a small opening
   that vision-shaped agent prose could try to enter through.
2. **Telos is essence, not Spirit.** Workspace ESSENCE.md holds the vision; Spirit
   holds only applied standing directives. Then `zn2l` moves to ESSENCE and the
   prompt stays strict. The applied design stances (`jys2`/`sj2c`/`cam8` to the
   extent each is "aim at the ideal in any design") stay in Spirit; the pure
   description leaves.

Either is coherent. I recommend resolution 2 (keeps the bar strictest and the
asymmetry cleanest; vision lives in ESSENCE where it already partly does), but
this is a psyche call, not an admission the Guardian should make silently.
